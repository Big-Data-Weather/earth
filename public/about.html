why

hurricane sandy | the case for Quotaero



Hurricane Sandy was predicted to touch down over 50 miles from where it actually did.

Now, to weather specialists. this number sounds great compared to what it would have

been 5 or 10 years ago, but to you and me it doesn’t sound as nice. Shouldn’t we be

able to provide weather predictions that can target zip codes, not land a hurricane

half a state away?

The problem lies in the fact that models are often wrong. But how do we fix a problem

that’s persisted for so long? We have to look at where mistakes have been made in the

past to see how this influences our present and can change our future.



As a solution, this tool aims to lay the groundwork for building a decision support system

for weather specialists that will compare predicted and actual weather of the recent

past in an effort to bringlight to the patterns of error currently residing in weather

forecasting systems.
